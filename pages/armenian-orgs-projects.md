# **Projects**

* **[The Armenian Language in the Bay Area Project (ALBA)](https://sites.google.com/view/juliannekapner/alba)**
{: style="margin: 0 0 8px 0; font-size: 1.5em;" }
<img src="/armenian-notes/assets/images/images/ALBA.png" alt="ALBA flyer" style="width: 100%; max-width: 500px; height: auto; object-fit: contain; border-radius: 4px; display: block; margin: 0 auto 12px auto; transform: translateX(-80px);">

The ALBA Project is directed and founded by **[Julianne Kapner](https://lx.berkeley.edu/people/julianne-kapner)**, a PH.D. student at UC Berkeley. The purpose of this project is to document and understand how the Armenian language varies and changes in the Bay Area and you can actually access the recordings and transcripts of the interviews the ALBA team has conducted through the [California Language Archive](https://cla.berkeley.edu/collection/?collid=11370).

From Kapner's website, she shares the 4 main goals of the project:

* To create a public archive/corpus of recordings of Bay Area Armenian for future linguistic, historical, and anthropological research;

* To create opportunities for UC Berkeley undergraduate students to engage with the Armenian language and culture through research;

* To understand how the Armenian language varies across social identities, and how it may be changing in the Bay Area; and

* To generate findings which can support and promote existing efforts to maintain the (Western) Armenian language.

This project will be highly beneficial to future studies of the Armenian language and I highly encourage you to consider giving it a look and contacting Julianne Kapner for updates and maybe even participating! The link to her newsletter is [here](https://berkeley.us10.list-manage.com/subscribe?u=7ef06c3bc48ad56d23211c7f7&id=8a42d9d2f3).
